Research

Sign-CAM: Leveraging Binary Weights and Activations for Efficient Interpretability in Binarized Neural Networks
Accepted ✓
XAI-2026 — 4th World Conference on eXplainable AI  ·  Fortaleza, Brazil  ·  July 2026

Gradient-based explainability methods such as Grad-CAM fail on Binarized Neural Networks (BNNs) because the Straight-Through Estimator (STE) produces unreliable gradients. Sign-CAM exploits the ±1 symmetric property unique to BNN weights and activations to determine each feature map's positive or negative contribution to the final prediction — entirely without gradient computation. It achieves Score-CAM-level explanation quality at significantly lower computational cost.

No gradient required Outperforms Grad-CAM / Grad-CAM++ Score-CAM quality, fraction of cost
Models: Binary ResNet · Binary VGG  |  Dataset: CIFAR-10  |  Baselines: Grad-CAM, Grad-CAM++, Score-CAM, RISE, BDD4BNN
Structural Pruning Without Losing Model Weights
Under Revision
Working Paper · Master's Thesis (NTU, 2025)

Proposes a criterion-agnostic post-pruning weight redistribution algorithm for structured (column) pruning. After unimportant columns are removed, the method learns how the removed weights can be approximated by the remaining ones through a linear regression step, then folds them back into the surviving weights. This initialization substantially reduces fine-tuning cost compared to naive pruning, grounded in Neural Tangent Kernel (NTK) theory. Compatible with any pruning criterion (L1, L2, Taylor, OBD, FPGM, Random) and validated across ViT, CNN (YOLOv8), and LLM (LLaMA) architectures.

One-shot ≈ Iterative pruning quality 3.27×–12.58× speedup Recovers divergent YOLOv8 pruning Criterion-agnostic
Models: ViT-Base/Large/Huge · YOLOv8 · LLaMA  |  Datasets: CIFAR-100 · iNaturalist-2019
