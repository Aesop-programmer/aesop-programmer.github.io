Structural Pruning without Losing Model Weights
Under Review
ICML 2026 (Submitted January 2026)

We propose WeightCompression, a post-pruning weight redistribution algorithm that is criterion-agnostic — it works on top of any existing pruning criterion (L1, L2, Taylor, OBD, FPGM, Random). After structured pruning removes columns from a weight matrix, WeightCompression solves a linear regression (Recombine) to learn how pruned weights can be approximated by remaining ones, then folds them back (Redistribute): W'' = W_R + W_P · A. Grounded in Neural Tangent Kernel (NTK) theory, this initialization substantially reduces the fine-tuning cost compared to naive pruning.

One-shot ≈ Iterative pruning 3.27×–12.58× speedup +2–13% accuracy on ViT-Base/CIFAR-100 Recovers divergent YOLOv8 pruning
Models: ViT-Base / Large / Huge · YOLOv8 · LLaMA  |  Datasets: CIFAR-100 · iNaturalist-2019 · ImageNet  |  Baselines: SlimGPT, OBC
Sign-CAM: Leveraging Binary Weights and Activations for Efficient Interpretability in Binarized Neural Networks
Accepted ✓
XAI-2026 (4th World Conference on eXplainable AI · Fortaleza, Brazil · July 2026)

Grad-CAM and related gradient-based methods fail on BNNs due to the Straight-Through Estimator (STE) producing unreliable gradients. We propose Sign-CAM, which exploits the ±1 symmetric property of BNN weights and activations to determine each feature map's positive or negative contribution to predictions — without any gradient computation. Sign-CAM achieves Score-CAM-level quality at a fraction of the computational cost.

No gradients needed Outperforms Grad-CAM/Grad-CAM++ Score-CAM-level quality, much cheaper
Models: Binary ResNet · Binary VGG  |  Dataset: CIFAR-10  |  Metrics: Avg Drop · Confidence Increase · Insertion · Deletion  |  Baselines: Grad-CAM, Grad-CAM++, Score-CAM, RISE, BDD4BNN
